  Ladakh — The Roof of the World
🏔️
Khardung La — Top of the World
Rode to one of the world's highest motorable roads at over 18,000 feet. The air is thin, the views are impossible, and your body reminds you very clearly that you are not supposed to be this high up.
18,379 ft above sea level
🏞️
Pangong Lake — On the Edge of Two Countries
Pangong stretches across the India–China border. Standing there at sunset, watching the water change from blue to green to silver — it hits different when you realise this lake doesn't care about borders.
4,350m · 134 km long
🏡
Village Man — Where the World Ends
Near Pangong, the village of Man is one of the most remote places I've ever stepped foot in. A handful of houses, no signal, no traffic — just mountains, silence, and the warmest hosts you'll ever meet.
Most remote I've been
  Spiti Valley — The Adventure That Tested Everything
No Signal. No People. No Problem.
Spiti Valley · Solo Ride
For stretches that lasted hours, there was nothing — no mobile signal, no other vehicles, no signs of human life. Just you, the bike, and 500-metre drops on one side. You stop overthinking things real fast when that's your only option.
The Water Crossing That Should Have Ended the Trip
Spiti Valley · River Crossing
Spiti has river crossings that aren't on any map. The water is glacial, the current is stronger than it looks, and your bike is not waterproof. We made it across. Barely. There were a few moments where "turning back" felt like the sensible call — but sensible wasn't what we were there for.
Midnight. Stuck. Towing. And Still Smiling.
Spiti Valley · Night Ride
The bike gave up halfway up a pass after sunset. We towed it in the dark on roads that had no guardrails and barely any width. We reached the guesthouse well past midnight, soaked and exhausted, and had the best chai of our lives. Some of the best trips aren't the ones that go to plan.
⚡
The Roads
Spiti roads are not roads — they're suggestions. Loose gravel, blind corners, single-lane paths cut into cliffsides. Every kilometre earns your respect.
Earned every metre
🌌
Nights in the Valley
No light pollution, no sound. Spiti at night has the kind of sky that makes you feel small in the best possible way. Stars so dense it looks like someone spilled milk across the sky.
Best skies I've seen
🤝
The People
In the most remote villages, complete strangers offered food, shelter and help without being asked. That generosity — no transaction, no reason — stays with you longer than any photo.
Changed my perspective
